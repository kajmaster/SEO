# SEO page patterns

## Recommended long-form structure

1. `<h1>`: clear promise around the keyword and buyer situation.
2. Intro: explain the concrete problem, context, and why the page is worth reading.
3. `<h2>` Search-intent section: define the topic in practical language.
4. `<h2>` Problem section: what goes wrong without the right approach.
5. `<h2>` Solution/approach section: explain the method or service step by step.
6. `<h2>` Selection criteria: help the reader compare options.
7. `<h2>` Proof/trust section: use real supplied evidence; if missing, explain what proof to look for.
8. `<h2>` Process/implementation: describe what happens after contact or purchase.
9. `<h2>` FAQ: answer 3-5 concrete objections or questions.
10. `<h2>` Next step: natural CTA.

## Prompt-echo prevention

Do not include sentences like:

- “Maak een SEO-pagina over ...”
- “Deze tekst moet snel duidelijk maken ...”
- “De basis voor de inhoud is ...”
- “Voor wie deze pagina bedoeld is ...”

Convert briefings into reader-facing copy instead. If the briefing says “Doelgroep: MKB-teams”, write about the real situation of MKB-teams.

## Useful section angles

- Cost/risk: what determines investment, risk, timing, and expected value.
- Comparison: when option A makes sense versus option B.
- Implementation: what the first 30 days look like.
- Objections: why teams hesitate and how to decide responsibly.
- Internal buy-in: how to explain the decision to management or stakeholders.

## Local service page additions

For local service pages, especially practical services such as woningontruiming:

- Name the service, place, and reader situation early.
- Add a short "wanneer nodig" section with recognizable triggers.
- Explain process, timing, access, cost factors, and what happens after contact.
- Include a trust section before the CTA.
- Add FAQ around cost, speed, service area, cleaning, proof, and what the client must prepare.

For ontruiming-specific pages, read `ontruiming-local-seo.md`.
